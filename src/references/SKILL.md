# Interview Prep Skill

A comprehensive, modular interview preparation system for candidates applying to multiple roles simultaneously.
Optimized for **Marketing / Brand / Growth** roles but adaptable to any industry.

---

## How to Use This Skill

When the user triggers this skill, first identify **which module(s)** are needed based on their request.
You can run multiple modules in one session. Always ask for missing inputs before proceeding.

### Required Inputs (collect if not provided)
- **Resume / key experiences** — ask the user to paste or summarize if not shared
- **Job description (JD)** — company name, role title, key responsibilities, requirements
- **Industry / company context** — stage (startup/MNC), culture signals, recent news
- **Interview stage** — phone screen, first round, panel, final, case interview

---

## Modules

### MODULE 1 — Customized Self-Introduction
*Trigger: user wants a tailored "tell me about yourself" or intro*

**Goal:** Craft a 60–90 second narrative that connects the user's background to THIS specific role and company.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook** — one compelling sentence about who you are professionally
2. **Career arc** — 2–3 sentences tracing relevant experience progression
3. **Why this role / company** — 1–2 sentences showing genuine fit
4. **Forward-looking close** — what you're excited to bring

**Customization checklist:**
- Mirror keywords from the JD naturally
- Highlight the 1–2 experiences most relevant to this specific role
- Adjust tone: corporate (MNCs like P&G, Unilever) vs. startup (growth hacker energy)
- For Marketing roles: lead with impact metrics, brand/campaign scope, or growth outcomes

---

### MODULE 2 — Core Behavioral Questions
*Trigger: user wants to prep standard behavioral questions*

Read `references/behavioral-questions.md` for full question banks and frameworks.

**Always cover:**
- **3 Whys:** Why this company? Why this role? Why should we choose you?
- **P&G 8 Questions** (also used widely beyond P&G): most stressful / greatest growth / proactive learning / communication / most challenging / leadership / teamwork / (add: failure/mistake)
- **Values & personality** questions (see Module 6)

**Answer framework — STAR+:**
- **S**ituation — set the scene briefly
- **T**ask — your specific responsibility
- **A**ction — what YOU did (emphasize personal agency)
- **R**esult — quantify whenever possible
- **+Reflection** — what you learned or would do differently (shows self-awareness)

**For Marketing/Growth roles, emphasize results like:**
- Revenue impact, conversion rate lift, CAC reduction
- Campaign reach, engagement, ROAS
- Brand equity metrics, NPS, awareness lift
- Speed to market, A/B test wins

---

### MODULE 3 — Role-Specific Question Prediction + Sample Answers
*Trigger: user shares a JD and wants predicted questions*

**Step 1 — Analyze the JD for:**
- Core competencies required (e.g., data-driven decision making, cross-functional leadership)
- Pain points the role likely solves
- Seniority signals (IC vs. lead vs. strategic)
- Industry-specific knowledge required

**Step 2 — Predict questions in 3 tiers:**
1. **Near-certain** — asked in almost every interview for this role type
2. **Likely** — based on specific JD language and responsibilities
3. **Curveball** — probing questions that separate good from great candidates

**Step 3 — For each predicted question:**
- Suggest which resume experience to draw from
- Draft a sample answer using STAR+
- Flag if the user should prep a specific metric or story

**If interview experiences are shared:** Summarize key themes, flag common question patterns, and use them to calibrate predictions.

**For Marketing/Growth roles, always predict:**
- "Walk me through a campaign you owned end-to-end"
- "How do you prioritize when you have 5 things to do and time for 3?"
- "Tell me about a time data changed your strategy"
- "How do you influence without authority?"
- "What's your process for understanding a new audience?"

---

### MODULE 4 — Resume Experience Deep-Dives
*Trigger: user wants to elaborate on a resume bullet or experience*

**For each experience, help the user develop:**

1. **STAR+ narrative** — full structured story (see Module 2)
2. **Problem → Solution → Result** arc — especially for projects with measurable outcomes
3. **Metrics & indicators** — if results weren't tracked, help construct a plausible measurement framework:
   - What KPIs should have been tracked?
   - What proxy metrics existed?
   - How to frame "I initiated the measurement system" as a strength
4. **Iteration story** — how did the work evolve? What did you change based on feedback/data?
5. **Your specific contribution** — distinguish personal impact from team/company impact
6. **Transferability framing** — how does this experience translate to the new role/industry?

**Depth prompts to ask the user:**
- "What was the hardest part of this project?"
- "What would you do differently now?"
- "What did success look like, and how did you know you achieved it?"
- "Who else was involved, and what was uniquely yours?"

### MODULE 5 — High-EQ Interpersonal Scenarios
*Trigger: user wants help with tricky workplace/people questions*

**Common scenario types:**
- Lack of cooperation from other departments / stakeholders
- Heavy workload / competing priorities from multiple managers
- Disagreement with a manager's decision
- Conflict with a peer or direct report
- Failure or mistake (own or team's)
- Managing up / influencing senior stakeholders
- Delivering bad news or pushback

**Answer principles for high-EQ responses:**
1. **Acknowledge** the difficulty without complaining — show empathy + professionalism
2. **Own your part** — even if the situation wasn't your fault
3. **Show proactive problem-solving** — what did you do to improve things?
4. **Demonstrate growth** — what did you learn?
5. **Stay positive** — never throw colleagues, managers, or companies under the bus

**Template structure:**
> "I've been in situations like that. What I've found works is [approach]. For example, [brief STAR]. The outcome was [result]. I've learned that [insight]."

**For Marketing roles, common interpersonal challenges to prep:**
- Creative disagreements with brand/design teams
- Sales or product teams deprioritizing marketing requests
- Attribution disputes (who gets credit for a campaign's results)
- Agency management — holding external partners accountable

### MODULE 6 — Values & Personality Questions
*Trigger: user wants to prep culture-fit, values, or personality questions*

Read `references/values-questions.md` for a full bank of questions and answer templates.

**Common question types:**
- "What are your core values?" / "Describe your work style"
- "What environment do you thrive in?"
- "How do you handle ambiguity / failure / feedback?"
- "What motivates you?"
- "Describe your ideal manager"
- "How do others describe you?"

**Answer principles:**
- Align with what you know about the company culture (research signals from JD, Glassdoor, LinkedIn)
- Be specific — abstract values ("I'm hardworking") are weak; tie to a concrete behavior
- Show self-awareness — acknowledge a genuine preference while framing it positively
- For weaknesses: choose a real one, show it's being actively managed, keep it non-fatal for the role

### MODULE 7 — Career Narrative / Story Arc
*Trigger: user wants to craft a cohesive story across their resume*

**Goal:** Build a through-line that makes career moves feel intentional, not accidental.

**Framework — The Career Logic Test:**
- Each move should answer: "Why did this make sense given where I was and where I wanted to go?"
- Gaps, pivots, or lateral moves need reframing (see Module 8)

**Story arc structure:**
1. **Origin** — what drew you to this field/function initially?
2. **Development** — how have your skills and scope grown?
3. **Inflection point** — what made you ready for / interested in this next step?
4. **Destination signal** — what does this role unlock for your longer-term direction?

**For Marketing/Growth candidates:**
- Connect experiences across the funnel (awareness → conversion → retention)
- Show progression from execution to strategy, or specialist to generalist
- Frame cross-industry moves as "bringing outside-in perspective"

---

### MODULE 8 — Weakness & Gap Handling
*Trigger: user needs to address resume gaps, pivots, or missing qualifications*

**Types of gaps to address:**
- Employment gaps
- Industry/function pivots
- Overqualified or underqualified signals
- Lack of a specific skill listed in the JD
- Short tenures or frequent job changes

**Reframing strategies:**
- Employment gap → intentional reset, caregiving, freelance, upskilling
- Pivot → "transferable skills + fresh perspective" framing
- Missing skill → "fast learner + here's adjacent proof" + show you've already started closing the gap
- Short tenure → company change / restructuring / strategic career decision, not a flight risk

**Principle:** Don't apologize. Acknowledge briefly, reframe confidently, redirect to strengths.

### MODULE 9 — Case & Situational Interview Prep
*Trigger: user is prepping for a case, strategy, or hypothetical scenario question*

For Marketing/Growth roles, common case types:
- **Go-to-market strategy** — launching a new product or entering a new market
- **Campaign brief** — given budget + objective, design a campaign
- **Growth diagnosis** — "Our CAC went up 30% last quarter. Walk me through how you'd investigate."
- **Prioritization frameworks** — rank these 5 initiatives with limited resources
- **Brand positioning** — how would you reposition this brand for a new audience?

**Case answer framework (MECE approach):**
1. Clarify the objective and constraints
2. Structure your approach (state your framework out loud)
3. Prioritize the most impactful areas
4. Make a recommendation with trade-offs acknowledged
5. Ask what the interviewer wants to dig into

**Always show:** hypothesis-driven thinking, comfort with ambiguity, data instincts even without data.

---

### MODULE 10 — Questions to Ask the Interviewer
*Trigger: user wants to prep thoughtful questions to ask at the end*

**Principles:**
- Ask questions that signal genuine curiosity, strategic thinking, and role fit
- Avoid questions answered on the company website
- Tailor to the interviewer's role (hiring manager vs. peer vs. exec)

**Question categories:**
- **Role clarity:** "What does success look like in the first 90 days?"
- **Team dynamics:** "How does this team collaborate with [sales/product/brand]?"
- **Company direction:** "What's the biggest strategic priority for this function this year?"
- **Culture signal:** "What do the people who thrive here have in common?"
- **Growth:** "What are the most common career paths from this role?"
- **Honest signal:** "What's the hardest part about this role that might not be obvious from the JD?"

**For Marketing/Growth roles, add:**
- "How is marketing's impact measured and attributed within the organization?"
- "How does the team balance brand vs. performance priorities?"
- "What tools and data infrastructure does the team work with?"

---

### MODULE 11 — Industry & Company Research Briefing
*Trigger: user wants to prep company knowledge before an interview*

**Research areas to cover:**
1. **Business model** — how does the company make money? Who are their customers?
2. **Recent news** — funding, product launches, leadership changes, earnings, controversies
3. **Competitive landscape** — top 2–3 competitors, how this company differentiates
4. **Marketing/Growth strategy signals** — what channels do they prioritize? What campaigns are visible?
5. **Culture signals** — Glassdoor themes, LinkedIn employee posts, leadership communications
6. **The role's context** — is this a new team? Backfill? Expansion?

**Output format:** Produce a concise "interview brief" the user can review the night before:
- 3 bullet points on business context
- 2 bullet points on recent news to reference
- 1 insightful observation about their marketing approach
- 2–3 talking points that connect company priorities to the user's experience

Use web search if the user provides the company name.

---

## Session Flow

For a full prep session, suggest this order:
1. Company/role research (Module 11) → sets context for everything else
2. Self-introduction (Module 1)
3. Resume deep-dives on 2–3 most relevant experiences (Module 4)
4. Behavioral questions (Module 2) + predicted role-specific questions (Module 3)
5. Interpersonal scenarios (Module 5) + values questions (Module 6)
6. Questions to ask (Module 10)
7. Career narrative polish (Module 7) + gap handling if needed (Module 8)
8. Case prep if applicable (Module 9)

For quick prep (1 interview, limited time), prioritize: Module 1 → Module 3 → Module 10.
