# Values & Personality Question Bank

## Core Values Questions

### "What are your core values?"
- Pick 2–3 values you can back with behavior, not just words
- Structure: "One of my core values is [X]. For me, that shows up as [specific behavior]. For example, [brief story]."
- For Marketing: creativity, accountability, customer-centricity, data-driven thinking, collaboration

### "Describe your work style."
- Cover: how you organize work, how you collaborate, how you make decisions
- Show self-awareness: acknowledge a preference, acknowledge when you adapt
- Example structure: "I tend to [work style trait], which I've found works well when [context]. In situations where [different context], I adapt by [adjustment]."

### "What environment do you thrive in?"
- Research the company culture first; align genuinely (not sycophantically)
- Include: pace, structure level, collaboration vs. autonomy, feedback style
- Show flexibility: "I do best in [X], and I've also learned to be effective in [Y]"

## Self-Awareness Questions

### "What are your strengths?"
- Pick 2–3 that are directly relevant to the role
- Each needs a behavioral proof point, not just a label
- For Marketing: analytical thinking, storytelling, consumer empathy, cross-functional influence

### "What are your weaknesses?" / "What are you working on?"
**Principles:**
- Choose a real weakness (interviewers see through fake ones like "I work too hard")
- Show it's non-fatal for this specific role
- Show active effort to improve
- Keep it brief — don't dwell

**Strong weakness structures:**
- Skill-based + actively improving: "I used to struggle with [X]. I've been working on it by [specific action] and I've seen improvement in [outcome]."
- Tendency + awareness: "I have a tendency to [X] when [trigger]. I've learned to catch it by [mitigation behavior]."

**Common good weaknesses for Marketing roles:**
- Perfectionism on creative output (show you've learned to ship and iterate)
- Public speaking / presenting to large groups (show active practice)
- Delegating creative control (show growth as you've moved into more senior roles)
- Saying no / protecting bandwidth (show you've improved prioritization)

### "How do others describe you?"
- 2–3 adjectives backed by examples or what people have actually said
- Mix: a professional strength + a people/interpersonal quality
- "My manager has told me I'm [X]. I think that shows up in [behavior]."

## Motivation & Career Questions

### "What motivates you?"
- Be specific and genuine — "making an impact" is too generic
- Connect to the role: what about this work specifically is energizing?
- For Marketing: seeing creative work drive real business outcomes, understanding consumer psychology, building brands people love, growing something measurable

### "Where do you see yourself in 3–5 years?"
- Show ambition without making it clear you'll leave in 2 years
- Anchor to skills and scope, not specific titles
- Connect to what this role enables: "This role would give me [foundation/experience], and I see myself growing into [direction]."

### "Why are you leaving your current role?"
- Keep it positive and forward-looking
- Never: bad-mouth current employer, sound like you're running away
- Always: frame as seeking something (growth, challenge, opportunity) not escaping something
- Honest but professional: restructuring, limited growth path, seeking [specific thing this new role offers]

## Culture Fit Questions

### "Describe your ideal manager."
- Be genuine but not demanding-sounding
- Include: communication style, feedback frequency, autonomy level, support vs. direction
- Show adaptability: "I've thrived under different styles — what I consistently value is [core thing]."

### "How do you handle ambiguity?"
- For Marketing/Growth roles: this comes up constantly; show comfort with it
- Structure: acknowledge ambiguity is real → show your approach → give an example
- "I've found that in ambiguous situations, my first move is to [clarify the goal / define success criteria]. Then [action]. For example, [brief story]."

### "How do you handle feedback?"
- Show: receptivity, non-defensiveness, ability to distinguish good feedback from noise
- Include: example of changing something based on feedback
- "I actively seek feedback because [genuine reason]. The most valuable piece I've received was [specific feedback] from [person]. It led me to [change]."

### "What does success look like for you in this role in the first 90 days?"
- Show you've thought about onboarding, relationship-building, early wins
- Generic: "Learn the product, meet the team, understand KPIs"
- Strong: "In the first 30 days, I'd focus on [listening/understanding]. By 60 days, I'd want to [specific early contribution]. By 90 days, I'd aim to [meaningful deliverable]."
