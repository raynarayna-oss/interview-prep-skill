# Behavioral Question Bank

## The 3 Whys

### Why should the company choose you?
Framework: Unique combination of [relevant skill 1] + [relevant skill 2] + [specific experience] that directly maps to what this role requires.
- Lead with the most differentiated thing about you relative to a typical candidate
- Include one specific result that proves competence
- Close with enthusiasm for contribution, not just the opportunity

### Why this company?
Framework: Specific + personal + forward-looking. Avoid generic ("great brand," "exciting company").
- Reference something concrete: a campaign, a product decision, a strategic move, an article
- Connect to your professional values or career direction
- Show you've done real research

### Why this role?
Framework: Role as the next logical step in your development + genuine interest in the function.
- What skills does this role let you build or deepen?
- What problems does this role let you solve that excite you?
- How does it connect the dots between where you've been and where you're going?

## P&G 8 Key Questions (+ Common Variants)

### 1. Most Stressful Situation
*"Tell me about the most stressful situation you've faced at work."*
- Show: composure under pressure, problem-solving, self-regulation
- Avoid: blaming others, catastrophizing, unresolved situations
- Strong close: what you learned about managing stress

### 2. Greatest Growth / Learning
*"Tell me about a time you experienced significant professional growth."*
- Show: self-awareness, coachability, initiative in development
- Best answers: growth driven by challenge or failure, not just a training program
- Include: what specifically changed in how you work

### 3. Proactive Learning
*"Tell me about a time you proactively learned something new without being asked."*
- Show: intellectual curiosity, self-direction, initiative
- For Marketing: learning a new channel, tool, emerging platform, or consumer trend
- Strong answers: learning that directly improved results

### 4. Communication Skills
*"Tell me about a time you had to communicate a complex idea to different audiences."*
- Show: clarity, audience-awareness, persuasion, listening
- Variants: "convinced someone who disagreed," "simplified something technical"
- For Marketing: presenting strategy to exec team vs. explaining performance to non-marketers

### 5. Most Challenging Situation
*"What's the most challenging professional situation you've faced?"*
- Show: resilience, judgment, resourcefulness
- Different from "most stressful" — focus on complexity and difficulty, not just emotional pressure
- Must include: how you navigated it, not just that you survived it

### 6. Leadership
*"Tell me about a time you demonstrated leadership."*
- Show: influence, direction-setting, inspiring others, accountability
- Works without direct reports: leading a cross-functional project, stepping up in a gap
- For Marketing: leading a campaign, aligning stakeholders, driving a creative direction

### 7. Teamwork
*"Tell me about a time you worked effectively as part of a team."*
- Show: collaboration, flexibility, contribution to collective success
- Nuance: your specific role + how you adapted to team dynamics
- Avoid: "we" without explaining your individual contribution

### 8. Failure / Mistake (common addition)
*"Tell me about a time you failed or made a significant mistake."*
- Show: honesty, ownership, growth mindset
- Structure: what happened → what you did wrong → what you changed
- Never blame external factors entirely; own your part

## Additional Common Behavioral Questions

- "Describe a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information."
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager. What did you do?"
- "Give me an example of when you had to adapt your plan due to unexpected changes."
- "Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a project or customer."
- "Describe a time you managed multiple competing priorities. How did you decide what to focus on?"
- "Tell me about a time you influenced someone without formal authority."
- "Describe a time you received critical feedback. How did you respond?"
- "Tell me about a project you're especially proud of. Why?"
